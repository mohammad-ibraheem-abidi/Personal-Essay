The Observer

It starts with noticing. A deep-seated awareness of the world's subtle mechanics, and the infinitesimal gap between what is and what could be.

"I have always envied people who can live without noticing themselves. It seems easier that way—less friction between who you are and who you could be. But I notice everything: the subtle shift of a bird’s wing, the drop in an engine’s pitch before it climbs, the infinitesimal gap between what I create and what I know it could be. I call it an affliction with its own rewards."

The Explorer

Embracing uncertainty not for the thrill, but because it forces growth. The future is not a path to follow, but an unknown to be solved.

"It pulls me toward interstellar travel because the vastness of space holds the ultimate unsolved problems. I’m fascinated by a spacecraft that uses inertia and electromagnets to harness solar radiation... I seek the unknown because it is the only place I am fully awake."

"I will live there—between disciplines, between answers, where improvement is always possible... because that's where the work worth doing begins."
